Hybrid Lenses

Hybrid lenses aim for the best of both worlds: they are made of a rigid centre housed in a soft 'skirt'. This way, they can provide the clarity offered by rigid contact lenses whilst still having the relative ease of use and comfort of soft contact lenses.

Advantages Disadvantages

Able to correct for irregular astigmatism like regular rigid contact lenses

Easy handling and comfortable like soft contact lenses

Good stability on eye

Minimally affected by foreign particles

Expensive

Less durable and shorter replacement schedule (~6 months) than other rigid lenses

More susceptible to dryness due to the soft skirt
